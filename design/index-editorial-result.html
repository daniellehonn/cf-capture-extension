Arriving slowly in Gion

The video opens here — the gentlest possible introduction to Kyoto. No temples, no crowds, just narrow lanes lit by lanterns and the occasional glimpse of a geiko walking to her appointment. Start here if you arrive Friday evening; the jet lag works in your favor when the city is asleep.

Temples before the crowds arrive

Set the alarm for 5:30 AM — yes, on vacation. The creator is unequivocal about it in the video: at 6 AM the famous orange torii gates are completely empty, and by 8 AM they're a moving wall of selfie sticks. The trade-off is worth the alarm.

A slow lunch in Arashiyama

The argument — made convincingly on camera — is that most people do Arashiyama wrong. They sprint through the bamboo grove, take five photos, and leave. The opposite version: arrive at noon, eat a long riverside lunch, walk the grove on the way back to the station when the light goes long. Half a day, not two hours.
